1. Collection of Information

1.1 Information You Provide to Us

We collect information you provide directly to us. This includes:

  • Account Information: When you create an account, we collect your name, email address, password, phone number, company name, and job title.
  • Payment Information: When you make a purchase, we collect payment card details, billing address, and other payment-related information.
  • Traffic Analytics Data: Information about traffic patterns, vehicle movements, intersection performance, and related metrics that you upload or input into our systems.
  • Communications: Information you provide when you participate in surveys, send us feedback, or communicate with us via email, phone, or other channels.
  • Profile Information: Preferences, settings, and other profile data you choose to provide.

1.2 Information We Collect Automatically

When you access or use our Services, we automatically collect:

  • Device Information: Hardware model, operating system version, unique device identifiers, network information, IP address, browser type and version, and mobile network information.
  • Log Information: Access times, pages viewed, IP address, and referring website address.
  • Location Information: Precise or approximate location information derived from your IP address or device GPS.
  • Usage Information: Actions taken, features used, traffic analysis preferences, and interaction with our Services.
  • Metadata: Information about how you use our Services, including click patterns and feature engagement.

1.3 Information We Collect from Other Sources

We may also obtain information from other sources, including:

  • Business Partners: Technical data from analytics providers, advertising networks, and search information providers.
  • Third-Party Services: When you connect your account with third-party services, we may receive information about you from those services.
  • Public Sources: Publicly available information from government databases, public records, and other public sources.
  • Traffic Management Agencies: Data from traffic management authorities and municipal corporations.

1.4 Cookies and Similar Technologies

We use cookies, web beacons, and other tracking technologies to collect information about you when you interact with our Services, including:

  • Essential Cookies: Required for the operation of our Services, including keeping you signed in and maintaining your preferences.
  • Analytical/Performance Cookies: Allow us to recognize and count visitors and analyze how our Services are used.
  • Functionality Cookies: Used to recognize you when you return to our Services and personalize content.
  • Targeting Cookies: Record your visits to our Services, pages visited, and links followed.

4. Data Retention

4.1 Retention Periods

We retain personal information we collect from you where we have an ongoing legitimate business need to do so (for example, to provide you with a service you have requested or to comply with applicable legal requirements). When we have no ongoing legitimate business need to process your personal information, we will either delete or anonymize it or, if this is not possible (for example, because your personal information has been stored in backup archives), then we will securely store your personal information and isolate it from any further processing until deletion is possible.

4.2 Retention Criteria

The length of time we keep your personal information depends on:

  • Legal obligations under applicable law to retain data for a certain period
  • Statute of limitations under applicable law(s)
  • Ongoing or potential legal disputes
  • Guidelines issued by relevant data protection authorities
  • Contract requirements with our clients and partners
  • Our legitimate business interests

5.1 International Data Transfers

We may transfer, process, and store personal information we collect about you in countries other than the country in which the information was originally collected, including the United States and other jurisdictions. Those countries may not have the same data protection laws as the country in which you initially provided the information.

5.2 Data Transfer Mechanisms

When we transfer your personal information internationally, we implement appropriate safeguards, including:

  • Standard Contractual Clauses approved by relevant data protection authorities
  • Data Processing Agreements with our service providers and partners
  • Binding Corporate Rules for transfers within our corporate group
  • Adequacy decisions issued by relevant data protection authorities
  • Consent mechanisms where appropriate

6.2 Security Practices

Our security practices include:
